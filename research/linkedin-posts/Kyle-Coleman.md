- **Date:** February 2026
- **Engagement:** 156 likes · 46 comments · 3 reposts

- **Why it matters:** Busts the myth that inbound hand-raises are guaranteed pipeline — the human connection is still essential even in automated funnels

Someone clicked 'Book a Demo' on your website. They were enriched + qualified. They picked the time and the meeting is on the books. 

🥺 They're a real opp... right?

In my experience, not quite.

After 13 years of managing inbound lead funnels, this is the biggest misconception I see. People think that a hand-raise = a guaranteed next step. That the sales cycle has officially begun.

Nope! Even when someone chooses a time + date from a nice auto-book experience… 20-30% of them no-show the meeting.

Not because of ill intent. But because there's a priority gap between how much they care about their day-to-day fires and how much they care about your product.

When conflicts inevitably come up, the vendor meeting is the first thing to go. Especially if they haven't interacted with a single human on your side. There's less impetus to show up for someone they've never talked to.

Same thing with free trials.

"They raised their hand! They're super high intent! How could they possibly need more help?!"

🤕 My personal record: I called someone 17 times over the course of three weeks before they actually started their free trial.

17 times. Again, no ill intent on their part – they just got busy. Life happened. Other priorities took over.

This is why I get nervous when people talk about "fully automated" inbound systems. AI that will "auto-qualify" and "auto-book" the next steps.

Those systems are missing the human connection. They're missing the salesmanship that's often required to close the priority gap and get people into the next step of the funnel.

A hand-raise is not a closed deal. It's not even always an opened deal! It's an invitation for you to earn someone's time.

It's a mistake to think you can automate that away.

- **Date:** February 2026
- **Engagement:** 72 likes · 23 comments

- **Why it matters:** Practical pre-call research framework that separates prepared sellers from lazy ones — 5 actionable steps

Your prospect Googled you before the call. The least you could do is return the favor.

Yet so many reps still show up and open with:

"So, tell me about your company…"
"What do you do?"
"Who are your competitors?"

⌛ Your prospect's time is precious. And you only get one first impression.

Every basic question you ask that could've been answered with 10 minutes of prep silently tells your buyer: "I didn't care enough to prepare for this."

I promise… buyers remember this sort of thing.

Show up well and you'll be rewarded. Not just with deals, but with trust, referrals, and a reputation that opens doors.

Differentiate not only your solution, but yourself as a seller and advisor.

–––

Here's 5 ways to take your pre-call research to the next level:

1. Find out what leadership is betting on right now. 

For public companies, skim the latest earnings call or annual report. For private companies, look at recent press releases, funding announcements, or interviews with their executives. Don't just know what the company does, know what they're prioritizing RIGHT NOW.

2. Check their job postings.

With a particular eye for roles that matter for your product or service. Open roles reveal where the company is investing and what problems they're trying to solve. A wave of data engineering hires tells you more than their About page ever will.

3. Study your champion's digital footprint. 

What have they posted, commented on, or shared on LinkedIn recently? What podcast were they on? What blogs have they written? Be sure to connect the dots between what matters to them and what you're slinging.

4. Map the buying committee before the first call. 

Use LinkedIn and org charts to understand who else will likely be involved in the decision. Walk in knowing the landscape, not just the person in front of you.

5. Know the competitive landscape.

Get a sense of the players in their space and the headline differences between them. See if any are already customers of yours and keep that in your back pocket. 

The bar is on the floor. A little homework goes a long way.

Show your buyers you respect their time, and they'll give you more of it.

- **Why it matters:** Real results from a team that 8x'd connected calls — breaks down exactly what drove the change across craft, targeting, friction removal, and AI

We 8x'd connected calls in 6 months.

Not just "dials." Actual conversations with real humans.

Real relationships still win. And relationships are built one conversation at a time.

Here's what drove the step-change:

🎨 Recommit to the craft of sales

Better talk tracks. Better discovery. Better objection handling. More coaching. More reps learning how to earn the next 30 seconds, not just "power through a script."

🎯 Make the right calls, not just more calls

We tightened targeting and prioritization so reps spend time where they can create value. No random acts of outreach, more intentional, account-aware conversations.

⚡ Remove friction everywhere

When you zoom in, productivity is death by a thousand paper cuts: clicks, tabs, duplicate entry, unclear handoffs, inconsistent workflows. We hunted those down and fixed them relentlessly.

🤝 Use AI to amplify humans (not replace them)

AI helps with research, context stitching, signal surfacing, and follow-up drafts. But the point isn't automation for automation's sake, it's freeing reps to do the highest-leverage work: build trust.

I'm incredibly proud of the XDR team. This trend is more than a chart, it's proof that the "human layer" matters more than ever.
