- **Why it matters:** Practical framework for coordinated team selling in B2B deals

Team selling boosts win rates by 50%. But most team selling looks like an 11pm uncoordinated salsa dance. 
Instead, assign your counterpart 1 of 4 roles:

1️⃣ THE MAIN EVENT: You run the bulk of the call (common for SEs and Product)

2️⃣ THE STORYTELLER: You use stories to elevate the conversation and engage power

3️⃣ THE BAD GUY: You have the hard conversation so the AE can protect goodwill

4️⃣ THE ROOM READER: You watch the room while the AE runs the demo

From there: My two favorite tag team plays are:

✅ The Question Elevator: 

The VP jumps in to go deeper on something, asks 1-2 questions, then DMs the rep "back to you" so the AE picks it up at a higher altitude.

✅ The Story Baton Pass: 

Before launching into a demo riff, you say "Our VP has a good take on this one," so they're ready when you pause.

- **Why it matters:** Highest engagement post — shows exactly how top reps handle the most common cold call objection

Not interested is the #1 objection on a cold call.

Here's how I handle it.

☎️ Angry Bill: "I'm not interested."

🤙🏽 Me (Agree With The Objection): 

"Shoot, my bad Bill. If you were, you probably would've cold called me while I was walking my dog in a fugue state."

🤙🏽 Me (Incentivize Conversation): 

"Just so no one calls you again, could you be brutally honest with me... 

Is it that you've got a solution, this isn't a priority, or you think I should go back to bootcamp for my crappy pitch? It's okay if it's #3!"

‍☎️ Angry Bill: "Ha. Alright kid. It's because I've got a million and one things going on."

🤙🏽 Me (Get Permission Again): 

"I get it Bill. My guess is this is a long shot, but I *actually* took the time to research you. 

Can I take 30 seconds to share why I wanted to reach out to you specifically, then you can totally tell me to kick rocks if it's not a fit?"

***

Remember: Not interested is not a real objection. 
It's a swat.
Agree with it to release the pressure of the sale.
Ask a question to reveal the real objection.
Then handle the real objection.

- **Why it matters:** Scalable personalization framework for SDRs and AEs doing outbound

Fake personalization is worse than NO personalization.

There are far better ways to personalize at scale:

(ps: this formatting is not AI it just makes sense for my brain)


=== 1: Pick a trigger bucket ===

Ask: What was my customer doing before they bought? 

My old client sold procurement software and the first thing a procurement hire would usually do was bring in vendor management software. 

That was their trigger. 


=== 2: Use this 4-sentence email framework ===

Personalization (what I saw about you)
Problem (what problem that creates)
Solution (how I solve that problem)
Offer (why you should take the meeting even if you never buy)


=== 3: Create a template for each trigger ===

Why rewrite the same email over and over again? 


=== 4: Add one "human thing" before you send it ===

Some of my favorites are:

- a humanizing PS (ps: your take on pizza is wrong)
- a screenshot of something from LinkedIn
- an intentional typo then immediate reply (don't abuse this)

Takes just as much time as copy pasting this dm I received.

## Post 4
- **Date:** March 2026
- **Engagement:** 135 likes · 18 comments · 2 reposts
- **Post URL:** [View on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_on-a-cold-call-your-tone-matters-far-more-activity-7432852163648720896-pah1)
- **Why it matters:** Addresses the mindset and inner game of cold calling — often ignored but critical for performance

On a cold call: Your tone matters far more than the words you say.

Top reps master the 'inner game' of cold call confidence. That means:

1. Picking talk tracks that feel natural to you (even if they perform worse)

2. Standing up and moving around as you dial (wireless headsets are a must)

3. Dialing in a bull pen with friends to laugh off nasty objections.

4. Matching their ridiculous objections with your own ridiculous reactions.

5. Saying "well clearly I'm sucking today" when you stumble on your words.

6. Realizing the goal of a cold call is not to book a meeting, but it's to break the bs corporate pleasantries and get the person to realize you're a human.

When you start having fun on the phone, you will book more meetings.

You don't need to be perfect. 

(in fact, being too perfect makes you corporate and easier to hang up on)

You just have to reveal the human behind the phone.

Here's a 2 minute and 59 second video on how we sent 6,474 personalized cold emails.

Five steps:

1. Pull the list

We pulled 224,000 contacts between our LinkedIn connections and newsletter following.


2. Narrow the list to the ICP

Chris Viglietta the Clay wizard helped us narrow the list to US-based sales leaders with 10+ reps on their team.


3. Narrow the list to the ENGAGED LEADS

From there, we drilled down to leaders who had 3+ reps who had ENGAGED with 30MPC content (so we knew their team was already looking at our stuff).


4. We used AI to writ personalized emails.

We plugged in 4 of our best cold email newsletters and 2 example templates into Clay's AI email writer, then had it personalize the emails based on the type of content the team had consumed.


5. Load the emails into the sequence (without getting spam blocked)

We created 20+ email domains and warmed them up for two weeks, then loaded them into Clay's sequencer to space out the sends over ~4 weeks so we didn't get knocked down by spam.
