Alright… let's make this real for a second

You've realized it's not your SDRs
You can see the system is off

Now you're sitting there like…

"Cool Tom… but I've got 30–45 days to fix this before Q2 is cooked… what do I actually do?"

because doing "more" isn't going to save you

I got you

Here's where I'd start if I walked into your org today 👇

1. Go talk to your last 10 CLOSED deals

Not your pipeline
Not your guesses

Actual customers

Ask

- what was going on in your world before you bought?

- what made it urgent?

- what were you trying before us?

You're looking for patterns

That's your real ICP
not a title

2. Sit your SDRs with Customer Success for 1 hour

No scripts
No sequences

Just listen to real conversations

How customers talk
How they describe problems
What they actually care about

Most SDRs are selling a problem the buyer doesn't even recognize


3. Rewrite your messaging in ONE sentence

If your SDR can't say it simply… it's broken

Try this

"we help [who] fix [specific problem] when [trigger happens]"

If that doesn't make someone go
"ya… that's us"

You're not ready to scale anything

4. Run live call coaching (not KPI reviews)

Don't ask

"how many calls did you make?"

Ask

"where did the convo break?"

Listen to 3–5 real calls as a team

Pause it
Workshop it
Fix it live

That's how reps actually get better


5. Cut half your sequences

Yes I said it

If your system is off
more volume just hides the problem

Focus on fewer, better touches
with messaging that actually lands

6. THEN layer in AI/scale

Not before

After you hear

"this is relevant"
"timing is interesting"
"tell me more"

Then you pour gas on it


This is the work almost no team wants to slow down and do…

Here's the truth

You don't have a 30–45 day "activity" problem

You've got a clarity problem

Once your team knows

- who they're actually talking to
- what problem matters
- how to talk about it

everything speeds up

Fix that…

and Q2 changes fast

## Post 2
- **Date:** December 2025
- **Engagement:** 27 likes · 19 comments · 1 repost
- **Post URL:** [View on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomslocum_most-sales-teams-think-cold-email-is-broken-activity-7386065013553418240-FSEl)
- **Why it matters:** Introduces the concept of "Inbox Theater" — outreach that looks like effort but reads like noise — and gives 3 principles that actually work

Most sales teams think cold email is broken

It's not their approach is 🤷‍♂️

Every week I see the same thing

Reps chasing clever subject lines

Over personalizing with fluff that adds no value

Asking for 30 minutes before they've earned 30 seconds of trust

We call this Inbox Theater 

outreach that performs for the sender not the buyer 

It looks like effort but it reads like noise

Here's what actually works in 2025 (and will still crush in 2026)

1️⃣ Blend in, don't stand out

3 words, lowercase, looks like a note from a peer not a campaign

"quick idea"
"team ramp check"

2️⃣ Start in their world

If your first line starts with "I" you already lost.
Lead with a trigger, change or challenge they actually care about

3️⃣ Earn the reply before asking for time

Show quick peer proof "helped [similar company] cut downtime by 25%" then close light "want me to send the 1 pager?"

That's it

No hacks
No magic words

Just simple, human relevance that cuts through the noise 

I've sent six figures worth of cold emails in the last three years across 40+ clients

This approach keeps winning. In SaaS, services and everything in between

Cold email isn't dead
It's just allergic to bad habits

## Post 3
- **Date:** 2025
- **Engagement:** 72 likes · 32 comments · 3 reposts
- **Post URL:** [View on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tomslocum_every-word-in-your-cold-email-is-costing-activity-7267231179983007746-w-cy)
- **Why it matters:** The $100 cold email budget framework — forces reps to treat every word as a cost and cut everything that doesn't earn its place

Every word in your cold email is costing you $1. You've got a $100 budget. Could your email pass the test?

Let me tell you I used to be the worst cold emailer 😬

Back in the day I was all about the mass send. No personalization, no strategy. Just spray and pray. 

And guess what? It didn't work 😆

But after sending hundreds of thousands of emails and finally figuring it out here's what changed

- 𝗦𝗵𝗼𝗿𝘁 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘀𝘄𝗲𝗲𝘁 𝘄𝗶𝗻𝘀. Nobody's got time for a novel. Keep it under 70 words—get to the point fast! 
 
- 𝗥𝗲𝗹𝗲𝘃𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲 > 𝗽𝗲𝗿𝘀𝗼𝗻𝗮𝗹𝗶𝘇𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻. Forget about tying in their dog's name unless it's actually relevant. Focus on why they should care
 
- 𝗕𝗲 𝗵𝘂𝗺𝗮𝗻. Drop the corporate buzzwords and write like you'd talk to a friend. Real conversations get real replies
 
- 𝗣.𝗦. 𝗳𝗼𝗿 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝘄𝗶𝗻. A quick benefit, a lighthearted note or even a small reminder can double your chances of a reply
 
- 𝗧𝗲𝗹𝗹 𝘀𝘁𝗼𝗿𝗶𝗲𝘀. Buyers don't care about your features—they care about results. Share how you've helped others like them
 
Now my cold emails land me 5-10% reply rates and we're talking positive replies not 'unsubscribe' or 'stop emailing me' 

The takeaway? Treat your words like they cost you. Make every single one count. And remember cold email isn't about selling—it's about starting a conversation 🤘

What's been your best tip for cold email success? Let's trade notes in the comments!
