- **Why it matters:** Practical 3-step AI workflow using careers pages to identify pain points and personalize outbound — a competitive edge most teams aren't using yet

This AI strategy helped me book 5 target accounts last month and it all started with the careers page.

Leaders.. think about this one.. a company's careers page gives you everything you need to connect the dots. 

I broke this down on a call with an AE team inside Sales Team Six and the reaction was immediate. Everyone was like yeah ok this is something that we needed.

So here's the play you can execute with your team:

Step 1. Figure out what job titles signal someone needs your product. For me... if a company is hiring AEs with "self-source pipeline" in the description, they need help generating pipeline. If they are hiring SDRs at a high clip, those reps might need development... Thats what our sales coaching program solves.

Step 2. Paste this prompt into Claude:

"Your assignment is to search the internet and far wide on sites like Indeed, Glassdoor, LinkedIn Jobs, and BuiltIn for companies currently hiring [YOUR JOB TITLES]. For each company, analyze the job description and tell me: what pain point it reveals, who the decision maker is, and give me 3-5 observations per account that I can use in an outbound message. It needs to be relevant and personalized to them. Build it as a spreadsheet. Now explain this all back to me so I know you understand the assignment"

Step 3. Screenshot the job posting and send it in a LinkedIn DM to the decision maker. The screenshot is your proof that a human actually looked and it's a 100% deliverable rate... No AI is connecting a careers page to a personalized DM... because it can't connect the dots like that. That's the whole advantage.

The teams using this workflow are connecting dots their competitors can't see. That's the game right now. 

Not who has the best AI. 

Who connects the best dots.

- **Why it matters:** Complete 5-step social selling system that an SDR used to book 17 meetings in one month — including 7 billion dollar companies and 42% of quota from LinkedIn alone

An SDR took my social selling system to book 17 meetings in one month. Here is the 5-step process you can emulate.

(Share this in your Slack group)

Step 1: Identification

Before we send any messages, we need to find the low hanging fruit.

The fastest way to figure this out is by doing the following.

- Go to your competitors site and see who they sell to
- Looking for new exec hires that have been there for 4 months
- Finding evaluators who were past champions or closed lost opportunities

The key here is that we need an unique observation every time we message someone or create a piece of content.

Step 2: Engage

If someone is active on LinkedIn, you need to be leaving comments.

Not basic ones like "Great Post" meaningful comments that are at least 1-2 sentences long.

If they don't have content, do this:

- Comment on posts they like
- Reply to comments they leave on other posts
- View their profile and hit follow so they are notified

Show that you are active so they can easily engage with you.

Step 3: Connect

For higher acceptance rates, send a blank connection request. I know I know sounds like a wild take. Yet in all Sales Team Six programs this has led to a higher acceptance rates. 

If they don't accept the blank connection request, this is the next step:

- Withdraw your connection request
- Send a new connection request in 3 weeks
- Use my direct note or give for content frameworks (in comments)

Our goal here is to get as many accepted connection requests in our ICP. Plus the more mutual connections the better. 

Step 4: Conversations

As the great philosopher Yo Gotti said, "It Goes in the DM." (Does anyone break out in a dance when they hear this or is it just me?)"

Don't jump into the conversation with zero context when you talk to people though. 

Find an observation about the person to make it relevant and personalized.

- Send a video message
- Shoot over a voice note
- Ask a persona-based question to spark a conversation

For example, on the written-based message you can ask, "Super random I know.. looks like you brought in Sam and Trent on the SDR team recently. Btw.. curious.. how are your sellers consistently executing a social selling strategy that leads to quality meetings?"

Step 5: Move to a Call

I am not here to chop it up all day long. It's not a barbershop. 

I am looking to build pipeline and start conversations with potential clients. 

Once you find a potential pain point in the conversation, we want to move this conversation off the platform.

- Offer a booking link so they can book time
- Send three times that might work for them
- Ask for their calendar link

Make this part frictionless so it isn't a week-long back and forth to schedule a call.

- **Why it matters:** 3 simple structural changes that doubled an SDR team's cold call conversion rate in just three weeks

An SDR team came to me struggling to book meetings on the phone. Three weeks later, their conversion rate doubled. Here are the 3 things I changed.

(save this for later)

1. Cold calling hour blocks

This sounds simple... but go look at your reps' calendars right now. Most don't have them. I didn't either when I started out as an SDR but this one tweak changed the game for me. 

Every rep should have at least 2 one-hour cold calling blocks. If cold calling is your highest-performing channel, make it 3.

This isn't about time management. It's about mindset. When reps know exactly when they're calling, they stop dreading it and start executing. The more organized the schedule, the better the results.

2. Write down your objections

When I was a rep and got an objection I didn't like, I would just hope they would never say it again. That's living in fear and fear doesn't book meetings. 

So I started writing down every objection. That is right Bobby.. every single one. Then I wrote out exactly how I'd handle each one. I practiced them until they felt natural and I had the confidence to handle it in real time. 

3. Know your stories

When I led my SDR team, I told every rep to memorize 3 customer stories.

Thing is.. buyers don't trust us. I know sad story. They will trust the customer stories that we share though. When you can pull someone in emotionally with a real story, they stop objecting and start listening.

Know your stories cold and you'll be surprised how many more meetings you book.

Leaders... the team already has the talent. Give them the structure and implement these three this week.

- **Why it matters:** Introduces observation-based prospecting as the next evolution beyond signal-based — the edge is not finding signals but knowing what to do with them

Last week the CEO of HubSpot posted "RIP cold outreach. Welcome signal-based prospecting."

I agree. A lot of us as leaders in the outbound community have been living this for years.

Signal-based prospecting isn't the "new era." It's table stakes. Most of my clients are already locked in and tapping into this.

I don't want to create another buzzword just to create one and I don't think it's revolutionary.. but the way I've been thinking about the next layer is observation-based prospecting.

Here's my point:

Finding signals is easy. Common Room, Pocus, ZoomInfo, 6sense... everyone has access to intent data now. It's as accessible as walking into a bank and grabbing the lollipops. (I make excuses to go the bank to get some because they're so good.)

Anyways, what's hard is connecting the dots to leverage WHY that signal matters.

Most reps see a funding signal and send this:

"Hey congrats on the funding! Looks like you have money now. Are you opposed to a 30-minute meeting?"

My friends... that is NOT going to work.

Messaging like that is the fastest way to the garbage bin.

Here's observation-based prospecting:

"Saw the funding press release and in paragraph 3 your CEO said that you're hiring 100 SDRs next quarter. That's a lot of onboarding. Just worked with a cybersecurity team to get their new SDRs booking meetings right out of bootcamp through cold calling and social selling on LinkedIn. Open to hearing how we built their system?"

Signal → Observation → Context → Human connection.

That's the formula.

This is what I work on with clients at AMP Social observation-based prospecting that gets potential clients to compliment your outreach. 

Everyone can find signals. The edge is turning them into observations that actually mean something to the person reading it.

We're not messaging robots.

We're messaging humans.
