Here's something that helped me remain a little more relaxed and chill. When I cold called, I would remind myself of Major League Baseball players. They don't get on base most of the time. In fact, they strike out most of the time. I think even the best of the world only get on base about 36% of the time. And if you watch them play, it doesn't seem to phase them too much. Like, if they strike out, they're like, "Whatever." And they go to the dugout. And if they get on base, they're like, "Okay, whatever." And they get on first base. That's because they know striking out and getting on base are part of the same experience. It's the same thing with cold calling. Whenever I was focused on getting on base, I would be disappointed when I didn't, especially if I was making two or three calls and they didn't go well. But what I discovered was if I made 46 calls, I would always have at least two or three positive conversations would last two or three minutes and some would even book a meeting. And knowing that just made me relax a lot more because I would know that if I just made the dials, things would always work out for me. The muscle to work is to step up to the plate, swing 46 times or 47 times, and know that things will always work out for you if you just keep on swinging. Detach from the outcome.
